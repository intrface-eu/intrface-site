# Intrface Homepage PRD

## Page Name
Homepage

## Goal
Present Intrface as a coherent AI-native product company/studio, establish the portfolio thesis, and guide visitors toward flagship products and contact pathways.

## Primary Audience
- Partners and collaborators
- Prospective customers
- Technical evaluators
- Future hires

## Secondary Audience
- Press / observers
- Existing network contacts evaluating the broader Intrface ecosystem

## Key Message
Intrface builds AI-native platforms, products, and systems. AOC, Voyager, and Funda are flagship examples of a broader platform-minded approach to intelligent software.

## Page Job To Be Done
The homepage should help a first-time visitor understand:
1. who Intrface is
2. what kind of company it is
3. what products it is building
4. why those products belong together
5. what action to take next

## Required Sections

### 1. Hero
Must include:
- Intrface name
- concise positioning statement
- supporting paragraph
- primary CTA to projects or flagship product exploration
- secondary CTA to contact or learn more

Recommended headline direction:
- “Intrface builds AI-native platforms, products, and systems.”

### 2. What Intrface Does
Explain the operating thesis in 3–4 concise blocks such as:
- build products
- design systems
- operationalize AI
- create reusable platform foundations

### 3. Featured Products
Must feature:
- AOC
- Voyager
- Funda

Each project teaser must include:
- name
- one-line value proposition
- optional status label
- clear CTA to dedicated product page

### 4. Shared Platform Capabilities
Translate technical depth into capability language, such as:
- AI agents and orchestration
- persistent workflow systems
- multilingual experiences
- role-based platform architecture
- task and memory infrastructure
- realtime and knowledge-aware systems

### 5. Why Intrface
Explain why the company is not just a set of disconnected apps.
Must communicate:
- portfolio coherence
- shared system thinking
- reusable infrastructure
- long-term platform approach

### 6. Trust / Proof Layer
Should include one or more of:
- screenshots
- architecture cues
- OSS/public repo links where appropriate
- product artifacts
- implementation signals

### 7. Final CTA
Should direct users toward:
- projects
- contact
- flagship product exploration

## Required Proof
- Real project names and accurate product descriptions
- No invented product categories
- Must reflect actual product work across AOC, Voyager, and Funda

## Required CTAs
- View Projects
- Explore AOC / Explore Products
- Contact / Get in Touch

## Must-Avoid Misrepresentations
- Do not frame Intrface as a generic agency homepage
- Do not flatten all projects into identical SaaS cards
- Do not overstate products beyond current repo reality
- Do not lead with vague AI language unsupported by the portfolio

## Content Model Needs
Homepage should consume:
- site-level company profile/config
- featured project data
- platform capability entries
- CTA config

## Design Notes
- Use Google Sans Flex as the primary typeface
- Maintain polished outcome-led product minimalism
- Structured, premium, high-signal layout
- Visual hierarchy should privilege product truth over decorative styling
- Editorial restraint is welcome, but conversion and comprehension come first

## Acceptance Criteria
- A first-time visitor can identify Intrface’s role and flagship products within one screen + first scroll
- AOC, Voyager, and Funda are correctly and clearly represented
- Page has strong CTA hierarchy
- Sections feel coherent, scalable, and reusable
- No section relies on invented technical jargon to create perceived depth
