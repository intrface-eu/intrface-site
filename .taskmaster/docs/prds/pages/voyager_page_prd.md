- Tourism agencies and destination organizations
- Travel platform partners
- Technical evaluators assessing platform sophistication
- Prospective collaborators and investors reviewing Intrface flagship products

- Local business stakeholders
- Curious travelers exploring the Intrface portfolio

## Key Message
Voyager is a next-generation tourism platform for destinations, businesses, and travelers that combines AI agents, discovery-focused mapping, multilingual content operations, and immersive exploration mechanics into one system.

## Page Job To Be Done
The page should help visitors understand:
1. what Voyager is and who it serves
2. that it is more than a travel app — it is a multi-tenant tourism platform
3. how AI agents, mapping, and content systems work together
4. what makes the Scout exploration model distinctive
5. why destinations, businesses, and travelers all benefit
6. how Voyager fits within the broader Intrface portfolio

## Required Sections

### 1. Hero
Must include:
- Voyager name
- concise platform framing
- clear supporting paragraph
- primary CTA to explore the product detail / contact / partnership path
- secondary CTA to architecture or product overview

Recommended framing:
- AI-powered tourism platform
- destination + business + traveler ecosystem
- exploration and discovery over passive tourism

### 2. What Voyager Is
Must explain the platform in audience terms:
- for destinations/agencies
- for businesses
- for travelers/scouts

This section must reduce technical sprawl and make the product legible quickly.

### 3. Multi-Agent System
This is a required signature section.
Must accurately present the three primary agents:
- Voyager — the guide / regional and agency-level intelligence
- Nexus — business-specific concierge / service context
- Guardian — attraction-focused historian / gamified experience layer

Must note:
- agents are domain-specific entities with configuration and knowledge
- context switching is route/entity aware
- RAG and structured knowledge are part of the system

### 4. Scout Experience / Exploration Layer
This is a required signature section.
Must capture the distinctive Voyager thesis around exploration.
Should include:
- gamified discovery
- map + compass + camera flow
- meaningful engagement over superficial tourism
- location-aware interaction and discovery

The page should communicate that Voyager is about turning travel into exploration, not just browsing listings.

### 5. Map and Geospatial Layer
Must explain:
- unified map system
- attractions / businesses / agencies as mapped entities
- discovery and navigation workflows
- location-aware context for users and agents

### 6. Content and Platform Operations
Must represent:
- multilingual content system
- scraping / ingestion pipeline
- content management
- role-aware platform operations
- B2B2C platform structure

### 7. Platform Structure / Stakeholder Model
Should explain Voyager’s ecosystem clearly:
- agencies
- businesses
- attractions
- users / scouts

This section should help the page feel like a platform, not a single-purpose app.

### 8. Product Proof
Should use defensible proof such as:
- multi-agent architecture
- map system diagrams or visuals
- multilingual support
- content management system references
- route/context switching examples
- platform entities and flows

No fabricated metrics.

### 9. Technical Architecture Summary
Should include a concise stack / systems summary:
- Next.js
- Convex
- WorkOS
- Mastra + Gemini
- vector search / RAG
- multilingual / realtime patterns

This must support credibility without overwhelming the page.

### 10. Final CTA
Should invite next steps such as:
- learn more
- discuss partnership / destination deployment
- explore Intrface projects

## Required Proof
- Three-agent system represented accurately
- Scout / exploration model represented accurately
- Multi-tenant tourism platform positioning
- Multilingual + content management reality reflected
- No invented category that hides the tourism domain

## Required CTAs
- Explore Voyager
- Discuss Partnership / Get in Touch
- Explore Platform Architecture

## Must-Avoid Misrepresentations
- Do not reduce Voyager to only a chatbot or travel assistant
- Do not present it as only a consumer app
- Do not omit agencies/businesses when explaining the platform
- Do not over-index on technical stack at the expense of product meaning
- Do not flatten the Scout experience into generic gamification language

## Content Model Needs
The page should consume structured product content including:
- overview and one-liner
- audience framing
- multi-agent definitions
- scout experience pillars
- platform stakeholder model
- proof / architecture references
- CTA links

## Design Notes
- Use Google Sans Flex as the primary typeface
- Voyager can carry more atmosphere than AOC, but still within Intrface’s outcome-led product minimalism
- Visuals should support discovery, navigation, and intelligence without becoming whimsical or theme-park-like
- The page should feel premium, exploratory, and platform-grade

## Acceptance Criteria
- A visitor understands Voyager is a serious tourism platform, not just a destination microsite
- The three-agent system is accurately described
- The Scout model is legible and differentiated
- The page clearly serves destinations, businesses, and travelers
- Product storytelling balances platform clarity with experiential appeal
- No invented claims replace documented platform features
